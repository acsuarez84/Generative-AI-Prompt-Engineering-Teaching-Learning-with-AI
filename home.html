Background
About the project
Teaching & Learning with AI

This project grew out of the challenge of helping multilingual writers engage more deeply with academic texts — and the observation that AI, when prompted carefully, can act as a genuine Socratic interlocutor rather than just an answer machine.

The Analytical Map externalises the invisible thinking moves skilled readers make: it asks writers to interrogate a text through time, language, rhetoric, genre, and authorial stance before forming their own argument. Prompting AI to inhabit each lens turns passive reading into active dialogue.

All tools here are built as static web pages — no server, no account, no data collected. They run entirely in the browser.

How to use these resources
For educators

Click any card to open the resource directly in your browser. Every resource is read-only and self-contained.

The Analytical Map works best when students paste a short passage (100–300 words) and then work through each concept node before writing their response.

The PDF is freely shareable — link it in your LMS, print it, or adapt the slides for your own workshop.

The source viewer is for the technically curious: you can copy the entire index.html and host it anywhere.
